MakeYourMap: Similar Address Flagging (SAF)

Reducing navigation errors in dense cities by flagging duplicate or similar street names at search time — with ZIP, borough, distance, and landmark cues.

Role: Product Manager Timeline: Jan–Feb 2025 Methods: PRD · Survey/Interviews · Personas · Wireframes · Agile
Jump to Solution See Research

The Problem

Duplicate or similar street names within the same city cause users to navigate to the wrong destination, wasting time and eroding trust. This issue was first surfaced through user complaints and support tickets, highlighting a recurring pain point for delivery drivers and new residents.

“I can’t afford to waste time guessing which Maximilianstraße the delivery is for.”
High impact Low–Medium effort MVP

Challenge: Quantifying the impact and identifying the most affected user groups required careful analysis of support data and direct outreach.

Objective

  • Reduce address-related navigation errors by ≥30%.
  • Increase user confidence & selection accuracy to ≥90% when flags appear.
  • Deliver a scoped MVP within two sprints.

Research & Insights

To better understand the problem, I conducted an online survey and follow-up interviews. Reaching a diverse set of personas was challenging, but the research provided actionable insights and strong storytelling value for stakeholders.

Methods

  • Online survey (n=16), demographics skewed EU urban.
  • Follow-up interviews across 4 personas.
  • Problem framing & PRD synthesis.

Solution: Similar Address Flagging (SAF)

Based on research, I worked with design and engineering to ideate and prioritize solutions. We chose to focus on an MVP that could be delivered in two sprints, balancing impact and effort. The team decided to postpone advanced features like Machine Learning recommendations to future iterations, prioritizing core detection and flagging for launch.

  • Detect duplicate/similar addresses within a radius.
  • Flag with ZIP, borough, distance, landmarks.
  • Guide users to the correct option with previews.
  • Control via a simple on/off toggle.

Planned OKRs for Post-Launch Evaluation

Objective 1
Reduce address-related navigation errors
  • KR1 Achieve at least 30% reduction in reported navigation complaints within the first 3 months after launch.
  • KR2 Reach ≥90% correct address selection rate when SAF flags are displayed.
  • KR3 Maintain a misnavigation rate of <5% for SAF-flagged addresses.
Objective 2
Increase user adoption of SAF
  • KR1 Achieve 20% adoption rate (users with SAF enabled) in the first quarter.
  • KR2 Maintain ≥75% feature retention among initial adopters after 3 months.
  • KR3 Generate at least 50 positive user feedback entries mentioning SAF clarity or usefulness.
Objective 3
Improve user trust and satisfaction
  • KR1 Raise relevant NPS score by at least +10 points among SAF users.
  • KR2 Increase task success rate in address selection flows to 95% in usability tests.
  • KR3 Achieve ≥4.5/5 average rating on post-navigation in-app surveys for SAF users.

Results & Learnings

While this was a simulated project, stakeholder feedback confirmed:

  • The problem was well-researched and clearly defined.
  • The feature design balanced impact vs. effort effectively.
  • Personas and research artifacts provided strong storytelling value.
Stakeholder Feedback
  • ✔ The problem was well-researched and clearly defined.
  • ✔ The feature design balanced impact vs. effort effectively.
  • ✔ Personas and research artifacts provided strong storytelling value.
Key Learnings
  • ★ Including user opt-out controls early can prevent future adoption friction.
  • ★ Persona-driven design made prioritization clearer for the MVP scope.
  • ★ Future iterations could add AI-powered address recommendations and manual reporting to improve accuracy over time.
