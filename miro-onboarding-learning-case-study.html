CASE STUDY
Miro: Onboarding & Learning MVP
Scaling engineering onboarding to improve productivity and reduce ramp-up time across five countries.
Role: Associate Manager, Engineering Excellence (PM responsibilities)
Timeline: 2023 – 2024
Methods: Discovery Interviews · MVP Scoping · Backlog Prioritization · Stakeholder Alignment · Agile Iteration

🔑 Case Study Snapshot

Type: Real-World SaaS Project (Miro)
Role: Product ownership responsibilities (discovery, MVP scoping, backlog prioritization, stakeholder alignment)
Problem: Engineering onboarding was inconsistent across 5 countries. New hires ramped up slowly, reducing productivity and creating friction for managers.
Objective: Build a scalable onboarding & learning MVP to shorten ramp-up time, improve new-hire productivity, and increase adoption.
Approach: Conducted discovery with new hires, engineering managers, and L&D leads → defined success metrics → scoped MVP learning platform → prioritized backlog → aligned cross-functional stakeholders.
Outcome: 🚀 Increased adoption · 📈 Boosted productivity · ⏳ Cut ramp-up time · 🌍 Rolled out globally.
The Problem
Miro’s engineering team was scaling quickly across multiple countries. Onboarding was inconsistent, leading to:
Long ramp-up times for new hires.
Reduced productivity in early months.
Frustration among managers lacking visibility into onboarding effectiveness.
Objective
🛠️ Deliver a scalable onboarding & learning MVP. 📊 Define and measure productivity and ramp-up success metrics. 📝 Improve the new-hire experience while easing manager workload.
Research & Insights
New Hire Interviews: Identified pain points in ramp-up and unclear learning paths.
Manager Feedback: Highlighted productivity lags and inconsistent role readiness.
L&D Collaboration: Benchmarked existing programs and identified gaps in scale.
Key Insights:
No single source of truth for onboarding resources.
Learning paths varied significantly across geographies.
Managers lacked visibility into new-hire progress.
Solution: Onboarding & Learning MVP
Scoped and delivered a lean MVP learning platform.
Features included: structured onboarding paths, progress tracking, and role-specific learning modules.
Defined measurable success metrics for adoption, productivity, and ramp-up time.
Ensured MVP was lightweight and scalable across geographies.
Prioritization
Applied MoSCoW and impact vs. effort prioritization.
Must: Central onboarding hub + progress tracking.
Should: Role-specific learning modules.
Could: Advanced analytics for managers.
Won’t (v1): Full integration with external L&D systems.
Outcome
  • 🚀 Increased feature adoption by 15%.
  • 📈 Improved new-hire productivity by 20%.
  • ⏳ Cut ramp-up time by 30%.
  • 🌍 Delivered a scalable solution used in 5 countries.
Results & Learnings
  • ✅ Demonstrated ability to scope MVPs that balance speed with scalability.
  • ✅ Reinforced importance of defining clear success metrics upfront.
  • ✅ Strengthened collaboration with cross-functional stakeholders (Engineering, L&D, HR).
Key Learning: Building onboarding at scale requires both lean delivery for quick wins and data-driven iteration to sustain adoption.
